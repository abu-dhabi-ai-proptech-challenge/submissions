# Judging Criteria

Projects are scored by a judging panel on five criteria, **equally weighted (20 points each, 100 total)**.

| # | Criterion | The question |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Problem & relevance** | Did you pick a real problem within your track and frame it sharply? |
| 2 | **Technical execution** | Does the prototype actually work, end to end? |
| 3 | **Use of AI** | Is AI doing real work — reasoning, matching, generating, deciding — or decorating the UI? |
| 4 | **Demo quality** | Did your 3 minutes land? Clear story, live demo, unmissable "aha"? |
| 5 | **Potential impact** | Could this matter beyond today — real users, extensible beyond the sample data? |

Per criterion: **17–20** excellent · **10–16** solid with gaps · **0–9** weak or missing.

## Process

1. Submissions close at the announced deadline. Only Issues opened in time are judged.
2. Each team demos for **3 minutes** + up to 2 minutes of judge Q&A.
3. Judges score independently; scores are averaged.
4. Track winners = top score per track. **Best Overall** = top score across all tracks.
5. **Best Use of Cursor** is awarded by the Cursor team for the most impressive development workflow.

## Tie-breakers

In order: Use of AI score → Technical execution score → judges' vote.

## Notes for teams

- Judges score what they see in the demo and the repo, not the roadmap.
- A narrow, working prototype beats a broad, broken one.
- Hardcoding a happy path for the demo is fine; misrepresenting what works is not.
